Linear Algebraic Equations

  • An equation is a mathematical statement such that one expression is equal to another
  • We solve equations by 'balancing'
Learning Objectives
  • Differentiate between expressions and equations
  • Solve simple linear equations involving 1 variable by balancing
  • Solve fractional algebraic equations
  • Construct equations from word problems and solve them algebraically
    • Balancing
  • In order to balance an equation,
  • Equal numbers may be added to each side
  • Equal numbers may be subtracted from each side
  • Each side may be multiplied by equal numbers
  • Each side may be divided by equal numbers
  • For example, to solve `7x+14=32-9x`:
    1)Add `9x` on both sides of the equation
    2)Subtract `32` on both sides of the equation
    3)Shift the number `46` on the right side
    4)Divide both sides by `16`
    • Fractional Algebraic Equations
  • When 2 numbers are equal, the numerators are equal IF the denominators are equal and vice versa
  • For example, `x/6 = 5/6, x=5`
